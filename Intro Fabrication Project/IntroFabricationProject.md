## Process
- I followed the Intro Fabrication Projects Instructions PDF on Learn.
- I started with the hinges as that was what I was given first in class. I used the horizontal bandsaw to cut my hinge to size. I then mapped out all of my cuts and holes with a sharpie and used the drill press to drill holes and vertical bandsaw to cut my angled part. 
- I then moved on to bending the tabs of the metal sheet template provided in class. I used the brake to accomplish this.
- I then created the lid by using the foot shearand brake again and also punched holes.
- I then had to make my two threaded tabs, cutting them to size first using the horizontal bandsaw and then the drill press and tapping station to complete the holes.
- After all of this was complete, I created the plywood insert using Onshape.
- What was left at this point was to assemple the box!

## What went well
- I think the process was a very fun process, being able to work with my hands felt rewarding as I was able to instantly and tangibly see the progress I was making. I think that cutting with the bandsaws was straightforward as well as the drilling of the holes. I was able to complete those tasks safely, effectively and accurately by measuring everything out first.
## What went poorly
- I think that the bending portion of the project was a pain point for me. I thought I was bending my metal sheet template correctly at first but ended up bending it a little over, causing some of the holes that I'd worked so hard to accurately drill on other pieces to no longer align perfectly. While most of the holes were fixable with a little manoeuvering and wiggling, one hole proved to be too difficult and I had to request the assistance of one of the shop supervisors to help me reach a solution. He was able to drill a piece of the hole out to fit the screw that I'd been trying to get in, but that was definitely a hurdle for me that could've been prevented had I been more experienced and aware of the way the metal in the brake can sometimes shift before bending.
## What you would do differently in the future
- In the future, I would definitely be more aware of the brake and how it can vary at times when bending sheet metal. I would line up my sheet metal more precisely and even have a friend come over to take a look before doing so. While I was being very precise with the other machines, I think the brake was looked at differently by a lot of the students as it was man/person-powered. I would definitely view it in the same way as machines such as the drill press and band saws in terms of the level of preciseness required when bending metal.
## What you are likely to use in your final project
- I'm likely to use the drill press as I'll probably need holes in some of my pieces to thread wires through and other connections.
